How to prove your identity at a Post Office

1. Enter your details on GOV.UK

You'll need to choose a photo ID you can take to a Post Office

Then you can enter your name, date of birth and current home address (and your previous address if you’ve recently moved).

2. Take your photo ID and customer letter to a Post Office

After entering your details, you’ll get a confirmation email and a customer letter from the Post Office.

Take your customer letter and photo ID to a Post Office that offers ‘in-branch verification’ within 10 days.

A member of Post Office staff will scan your photo ID and take a photo of you.

Not all Post Offices offer this service. You can find your nearest Post Office that offers in branch verification (opens in new tab).

3. Wait for your result

You’ll get an email with the outcome of your identity check - usually within a day of visiting the Post Office.
