You must have a photo ID to prove your identity with a GOV.UK One Login

You can only prove your identity with a GOV.UK One Login if you have one of the following types of photo ID.

UK and EU photocard driving licences

You'll need a valid full or provisional driving licence with your photo on it.

UK or non-UK passports

You can prove your identity with any type of passport, including biometric passports (or 'ePassports'). These passports have a small rectangular symbol on the front cover. You can use them to go through eGates at airpots and train stations.

If you have an expired UK biometric passport, you can still use it to prove your identity up to 18 months after the expiry date.

Identity cards

You can prove your identity with any of the following identity cards:

  • biometric residence permit (BRP)
  • national identity card from a European Economic Area (EEA) country
